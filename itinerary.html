Intramuros Itinerary

Historical Sites:

Fort Santiago

-It was built in 1593 by the Spanish conquistador Miguel López de Legazpi in order to protect Manila from several foreign invaders. It served as the main defense post of Intramuros.
-Fort Santiago is most known as the prison where Dr. José Rizal was kept before his execution in 1896.
-Entrance fee: ₱75 (regular rate), ₱50 (discounted rate)

San Agustin Church

-San Agustin church is known as the oldest surviving stone church in the Philippines, standing since 1607. With the use of adobe stones, the church is able to handle and survive several earthquakes that destroyed other nearby buildings.
-No entrance fee

Casa Manila

-This is designed to recreate the style of mansions in the Spanish era in order to show how upper-class Filipinos lived during those times.
-Entrance fee: ₱75 (regular rate), ₱50 (discounted rate)

Cultural and Educational:

Museo De Intramuros

-Modern museum showcasing religious art and artifacts from the Spanish period.
-This museum has six galleries across several floors. It displays plenty of religious art and objects used by Filipino Catholics during the Spanish rule.
-Entrance fee: ₱150 (regular rate), ₱120 (discounted)

Light and Sound Museum

-A museum that features a captivating presentation of José Rizal's life with the use of animatronics and lights.
-Entrance fee: ₱150 (per person)

Cafes and Restaurants:

Illustrado Restaurant

-Serves traditional Filipino-Spanish cuisine with a historic ambiance.
-Location: 744 Cabildo Street (corner Recoletos Street), Intramuros.
-Average meal costs: ₱800-₱1500

Barbara’s Heritage Restaurant

-Serves Filipino-Spanish dishes in a historic and traditional setting, often with live cultural performances in the evening.
-Location: Plaza San Luis Complex, General Luna St, Intramuros, Manila, 1002 Metro Manila, Philippines
-Average meal costs: ₱800-₱1200

Other Activities:

Bambike Tours

-Guided bike tour using eco-friendly bamboo bicycles.
-Cost: ₱350-₱500 (per hour)

Calesa Ride

-Traditional horse-drawn carriage.
-Cost: ₱400-₱500 (per ride)
